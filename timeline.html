The early pandemic was a confusing time

Mar 16

Tracking case counts

In the early days of the pandemic I decided to track case count. It felt pretty novel at the time, before most media outlets created COVID tracker web pages. At this point in the pandemic South Korea had the most cases and Italy was starting to grow rapidly. Case counts in other countries were comparitively very low.

I wanted to see how similar the growth rate of #COVID19 is between countries. Italy just passed South Korea's cases per population but the trajectory is starkly different. pic.twitter.com/jYuvAXEDGW

April

Mask-wearing becomes normalized

By April mask-wearing was becoming the social norm in San Francisco, despite no rules mandating it at this time. This was well in advance of masks becoming normalized in the UK. Here I'm wearing a mask we had saved for the aftermath of a possible earthquake.

Last weekend wore a mask to the grocery store and felt like it was a bit much. This weekend forgot the mask and it felt like the most reckless thing I've done since deciding to have a child.
